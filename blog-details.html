Q2 Logistics Yields Report

Published June 15, 2026 • Written by Marcus Vance • 8 Min Read

Logistics real estate continues to serve as the anchor of institutional property portfolios. In this quarterly breakdown, we look at the prime logistics cap rate movements across the EU and North American hubs.

Shifts in Hub Allocations

Port cities and major freight nodes in Rotterdam, Antwerp, and Hamburg have seen persistent tenant demand, stabilizing capitalization rates despite interest rate environments. Let's look at the yield indexing table below for representative corridors:

Target Region Prime Cap Rate YoY Change Occupancy Rate
Rotterdam Logistics Corridor 4.90% +15 bps 98.4%
Frankfurt Hub Node 5.15% +20 bps 97.8%
London Gateway Port 4.75% +10 bps 99.1%
Antwerp Freight Corridor 5.20% +25 bps 96.9%

Supply Chains & E-Commerce

The expansion of regional fulfillment grids has kept vacancy rates near historic lows. Institutional developers are focusing on CAPEX placement that targets automated, high-clearance warehouses rather than generic container properties.

"We anticipate that automated prime warehousing will command a lease premium of up to 12% over legacy industrial sites by late 2027."
